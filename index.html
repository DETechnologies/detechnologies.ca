Overview
This project aims to contribute to the global research field regarding Rotating Detonation Rocket Engine (RDRE) development. RDREs have prospective applications as orbital maneuvering thrusters, staged launch vehicle propulsion and supersonic aircraft engines. The goal of this project is to build a modular, liquid cooled, RDRE capable of thrust up to 1000N.
Our operational goals during the development process are to maximize prototype efficiency, develop an advanced ignition control system, and collect empirical data. The engine will be designed modularily, to accomodate future changes & technological advances, as research progresses allowing for optimal workflow. Work will be published in full, as a reference roadmap for future design, construction, and testing of RDREs; here at Memorial and on the global scale.
Long Term Objectives
DETechnologies is seeking not only to contribute to the research environment surrounding the development of RDREs, but also to stimulate aerospace/rocketry/combustion research interest in Newfoundland. With our successful RDRE prototype, we plan to:
  • Encourage the Memorial Univeristy Engineering Faculty to hire Aerospace-focused faculty members, and help establish a new research lab & research program.
  • Push for undergraduate Engineering aerospace/combustion courses.
  • Open opportunities for a student team at MUN to attend TheLaunchCanada student rocketry competition.
  • Showcase our functional RDRE prototype to inspire future Engineering students, and to promote Memorial's potential for future Aerospace Engineering success.

About the Team
Our founding members are four Mechanical Engineering Students studying at Memorial University of Newfoundland. Each have previously played key leadership roles on either Baja or Paradigm student design teams, all which have been important to Memorial Engineering programs growth. This project originally began as an Engineering Capstone project, where it inspired them to pursue RDRE research further. DETs mission is not only to sustainably power the future of Aerospace Engineering, but also to to inspire and empower students to push boundaries. "The sky is not the limit"
